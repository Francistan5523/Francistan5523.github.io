Software Talks: An Interview with Lev Manovich
Lev Manovich is a leading theorist in cultural objects
produced by Digital Technology
How does one study software? Or what do you study
- the interface or the code or a mixture of both?

The goal of my book is to understand media software

- genealogy (the where), its anatomy (the key features)

and its effects on the world;

How design software shapes the media, making some design choices

easy and natural whilst hiding others

How media viewing, managing, remixing software shapes our experiences

and our actions

The primary case studies are After Effects, Photoshop and Google Earth

I want to understand what the media is today. Do the concepts of media

developed for industrial era-technologies apply to video or do they

need to be updated?

Do we have different media or did they merge into a single new medium?

Motion, graphic design, web sites, product designs, buildings and

video games are all designed with software

Does ‘Media’ still exist?

It's about asking broad questions, not just code or interface.

Our world, media, economy and social relations all run on software.

Looking at historical precedents, what
extent were you able to engage with the
original software or documentation thereof?
To what extent were you relying on written
texts by these early figures

Theoretical ideas from 1960s and 1970s which led to modern media

authoring environments and common features of interface. Sutherland

and his collaborators demonstrated with Sketchpad - the first

interactive drawing system seen by the public.

There is overall little documentation of the key systems with small

black and white illustrations and just a few low resolution clips.

The situation is quite different from media technologies, you could

go to a museum to experience a real panorama from the 1840s. With

computer systems, most ideas come from contemporary media studies but

the original systems are not accesible. Though since there are a number

of artists and programmers who work in 'Software Art' and 'Creative Coding',

it should be possible to create emulations of a few fundamental systems.

An early concept in your book in Alan Kay's
concept "Dynabook", which posited a computer
as 'personal dynamic media' to be used by all.
It was brought into fruition in the Xerox Alto
computer, what importance can we attach to
these images of users, interfaces and objects
produced by these systems?

The most informative images from the Dynabook appears in an article also

written with Adele Goldberg in 1977. Kay suggested that computers can be

a medium for personal expression.

Alan Turing defined a computer as a machine that can simulate a large class

of other machines. It was Kay and his generation that extended the idea of

simulation to the media. In a book, it simulates existing media that has a

chance to add and extend new functions. For example, an electronic book

needs to be treated as a simulated paper book as it is a new medium with

new properties.

Many images of media software developed by Xerox team and other Alto users

illustrate these ideas, Kay and Goldberg provided examples of their Dynabook

that could allow users to paint, draw, animate, compose music and text. This

makes Alto the first Universal Media Machine, a computer offering the ability

to create experiences and artefacts in that sense.

I find it interesting your approach in analysing
specific elements of interface and operations.
Using the example of Sketchpad, was it not
documentation that allowed for the argument
that early software was not merely simulations
of drawings but reinventions of it?

The Sketchpad demo has visceral impact. Where does it lead with analysis of

Drawing, Film, Photography.

The properties of different mediums are properties of media includes:

Interface, tools and techniques they make possible for navigating, creating,

editing, and sharing documents.

For example, being able to change from Adobe Reader to Microsoft Word is not

the property of text documents, but a result of software techniques.

There are some media independent techniques such as the search bar, whereas

some media specific are exclusive for some. For example, you can extrude a

2 Dimensional shape to 3 Dimensions but not extrude text.

I prefer to study the software and use this understanding
in interpreting the content created through these medias
- tweets, messages, photos, designs, video games.

All cultural forms which require a user to click even once, to access

or participate. Therefore we can't ignore technology, therefore

software takes command.
